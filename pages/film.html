The Problem

If you're a UPM or production accountant in Vancouver, you know this:

  • 4 unions on every set — IATSE 891, Teamsters 155, UBCP/ACTRA, DGC — each with their own rate card, fringe structure, overtime rules, and 2025 increases. None of them align with each other.
  • One fringe miscalculation triggers a union grievance halfway through a 60-day shoot. There is no good time to handle this — especially not at 7pm during a night shoot.
  • The PSTC claim is due within 36 months. The documentation lives in 11 different spreadsheets, three accountants' inboxes, and a folder nobody can find on the shared drive.
  • Call sheets go out via email chains. A revised schedule at 6pm means someone works until midnight rebuilding them — and still misses a department head who was moved to a different unit.
  • Permit applications for 12 locations, all with different City of Vancouver lead times. One gets missed. You find out the morning of the shoot when a bylaw officer shows up before the crew does.
  • WorkSafe BC risk assessment for a stunt sequence written by hand at 11pm the night before. It's not that nobody cares — there simply isn't enough time in the day.
